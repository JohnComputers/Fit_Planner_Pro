Push Day

Chest, Shoulders, Triceps

    üéØ Form: Core tight, glutes squeezed, press straight up, full lockout at top
    üí™ Muscle: Primary: Deltoids (all heads) | Secondary: Upper chest, triceps, core
    ‚ö†Ô∏è Safety: Don't arch lower back excessively, keep wrists straight

    üéØ Form: 30-45¬∞ incline, full range of motion, control the descent

üéØ Essential Training Principles

üí™

Progressive Overload

The foundation of muscle growth. Gradually increase weight, reps, or sets over time. Aim to beat your previous performance each week. Track every workout!

‚è±Ô∏è

Rest Periods

Heavy compounds (1-6 reps): 3-5 min. Moderate sets (6-12 reps): 2-3 min. High reps (12-15+): 60-90 sec. Don't rush - full recovery = better performance.

üéØ

Perfect Form First

Master technique before adding weight. Poor form = injuries and suboptimal gains. Record your sets, get feedback, and leave your ego at the door.

üî•

Proper Warm-Up

5-10 minutes cardio to raise heart rate. Dynamic stretching for the muscles you'll train. 2-3 warm-up sets with progressively heavier weight before working sets.

üò¥

Recovery & Sleep

Muscle grows during rest, not in the gym. Get 7-9 hours of sleep. Take 1-2 complete rest days per week. Deload every 4-6 weeks (reduce volume by 50%).

üçΩÔ∏è

Nutrition Timing

Pre-workout (1-2 hrs before): Carbs + moderate protein. Post-workout (30-60 min after): Fast-digesting protein + carbs to replenish glycogen and start recovery.

üíß

Hydration

Drink 0.5-1oz water per lb of bodyweight daily. More on training days. Dehydration reduces strength by 10-20%. Bring water to every workout.

üìä

Track Everything

Log every workout: exercises, sets, reps, weight used. Track body measurements weekly. Take progress photos monthly. You can't improve what you don't measure.
